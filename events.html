Gifaataa Festival 2025

Celebrate Wolayta’s New Year with the vibrant Gifaataa Festival, a cultural extravaganza featuring traditional dances, music, and feasts. ... Held annually in Wolaita Sodo, this festival marks the Wolayta New Year with colorful processions, performances of the Wolayta flute and drum, and communal meals featuring ensete-based dishes like amicho and bulla. Visitors can join locals in sacred rituals under ancient trees, learn about Wolayta folklore, and experience the warmth of community spirit. The event also includes craft markets showcasing handwoven baskets and traditional attire, offering a deep dive into Wolayta’s cultural heritage.

Wolayta Coffee Harvest Tour

Join our annual coffee tour to explore Wolayta’s coffee farms, participate in harvesting, and enjoy traditional coffee ceremonies. ... This immersive event takes you to the lush coffee plantations of Wolayta, where you’ll learn about sustainable farming practices and the journey of coffee from bean to cup. Participate in picking ripe coffee cherries (season permitting), witness the washing and drying process, and savor freshly brewed coffee in a traditional jebena ceremony. Local farmers share stories of Wolayta’s coffee heritage, making this a perfect blend of culture, nature, and flavor for coffee enthusiasts.

Wolayta Cultural Performance Night

Experience an evening of Wolayta’s music, dance, and storytelling under the stars, celebrating the region’s rich cultural heritage. ... Held in Wolaita Sodo, this event showcases traditional Wolayta performances, including the energetic Yekokeb Berhan dance and soulful songs accompanied by the krar (lyre). Storytellers share tales of Wolayta’s royal past, including legends of Kawo Tona Gaga, the last king. Guests can enjoy local delicacies and interact with artisans displaying crafts like pottery and weaving. This cultural night is a vibrant celebration of Wolayta’s identity, perfect for visitors seeking an authentic experience.
